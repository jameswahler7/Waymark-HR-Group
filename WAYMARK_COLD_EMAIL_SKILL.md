## CORE PHILOSOPHY — READ BEFORE WRITING A SINGLE WORD

The goal of a cold email is **NOT** to book a call.
The goal of a cold email is **NOT** to get a click.
The goal of a cold email is to get **a reply.**

Any reply. Even "not interested" opens a thread.

Once they reply, you're in a conversation. Conversations close. Cold links don't.

**The Hormozi Truth:** You are not a salesperson emailing a prospect. You are a peer with a specific operational insight, reaching out to a busy owner because you noticed something specific about their business. Act accordingly.

---

## THE 5-PART BLUEPRINT — MANDATORY STRUCTURE

### PART 1 — SUBJECT LINE
- **Length:** 2–5 words maximum
- **Case:** mostly lowercase. Feels like a text from someone they know.
- **Rule:** Zero hype. Zero clickbait. Zero "I hope this finds you well" energy.
- **Goal:** Get opened. Nothing more.

**Approved formulas:**
- `quick question, [First Name]`
- `[First Name] / [topic]`
- `re: [Company Name] [topic]`
- `[First Name] — quick thought`

**Banned forever:**
- "Exciting opportunity for [Company]"
- "Partnership proposal"
- "I'd love to connect"
- Any subject with exclamation points
- Any subject with numbers promising results ("3x your hiring speed")

---

### PART 2 — THE HOOK (Lines 1–2)
- One sentence. Two maximum.
- Reference something **specific and real** about their business: location, industry, company size, years in business, active job postings, recent news, LinkedIn detail.
- **No flattery.** "I love what you're doing" is banned.
- **No generic openers.** "I hope this email finds you well" is banned.
- The hook answers: *Why are you emailing THIS person specifically?*

**Good examples:**
- "Noticed [Company] has been running HVAC and plumbing in Buffalo for 38 years."
- "Saw [Company] has three open fabricator roles on Indeed right now."
- "Running a 50-person construction crew in WNY puts a specific set of compliance requirements on your desk."

**Bad examples:**
- "I came across your company online and was impressed."
- "As a fellow WNY business, I thought you might be interested."
- "I hope you're doing well — I wanted to reach out about..."

---

### PART 3 — THE VALUE (2–3 sentences)
- Deliver a **specific, operational insight** about the pain they're experiencing.
- Use **real dollar amounts** or **real timeframes** wherever possible.
- Reference the **free asset** (protect.waymarkhrgroup.com) as a tool that exists — but do NOT paste the link here.
- The value should make them think: *"Hm. I haven't thought about that."*

**For compliance angle:**
- Reference NY-specific laws (LS-54, Construction Industry Fair Pay Act, Section 195 paystub requirements)
- Use real fine/penalty amounts
- Reference the free 90-second NY Employer Protection Scorecard

**For hiring angle:**
- Reference recruiter fee costs ($8K–$15K per placement)
- Reference time-to-hire benchmarks (industry average 42 days; your system: under 14)
- Reference the AI Hiring Accelerator as the mechanism

**BANNED words/phrases:**
- scale, double, triple, guarantee, risk-free, passive income, game-changer, revolutionary, cutting-edge, synergy, leverage, skyrocket, explode, crush, dominate

---

### PART 4 — THE CTA (1 sentence)
**CRITICAL RULE: Never paste a raw URL. Never ask for a meeting. Never ask for a 15-minute call.**

The CTA must do ONE of these two things:
1. Ask a **reply-generating question** about their specific business situation
2. Ask **permission** to send the asset link

**Option A — Reply-generating question (PREFERRED for first touch):**
- "When's the last time someone reviewed [Company]'s employee handbook against current NY law?"
- "Are you handling recruiting in-house right now, or through a staffing agency?"
- "Quick question — how long does it typically take you to fill an open [trade] position?"
- "Has anyone walked through your I-9 files lately?"

**Option B — Permission to send asset:**
- "Want me to send over the link? Takes 90 seconds and shows you exactly where [Company] stands."
- "Mind if I send the free check over? No email required to start it."

**The two-message sequence:**
- Email 1 → CTA is a question → they reply
- Reply 1 → "That's exactly what I help with. I built a free 90-second check for WNY [trade] owners — shows you exactly where your gaps are. Want the link?"
- They say yes → send protect.waymarkhrgroup.com
- They take the quiz → they're in the funnel

---

### PART 5 — THE PS LINE
- One sentence.
- Delivers authority, credentials, or **real scarcity** (not fake urgency).
- Never "Act now!" — always factual capacity constraint.

**Approved PS formulas:**

*For Touch 1 (cold open — real scarcity is the right tool here):*
- "P.S. I take 3 new clients per month. Currently have one opening for [Month]."
- "P.S. SHRM-Certified, 10 years in WNY HR. All work is hands-on — no outsourcing."

*For Touch 2, 3, and 4 only (follow-ups — proof of work beats scarcity here):*
- "P.S. Last WNY shop I audited had 3 handbook gaps that would've cost $50K+ to defend. They had no idea."

## FORMATTING RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

1. **Plain text only.** No HTML. No images. No fancy signatures with logos.
2. **Short paragraphs.** Maximum 2 sentences per paragraph. Single-sentence paragraphs are encouraged.
3. **White space.** Every paragraph separated by a blank line. Looks like a text on mobile.
4. **No hyperlinks in body.** Links go in reply #2, after they respond to email #1.
5. **Signature:** First name only, or first + last. Company name. Phone number. No LinkedIn badges, no image headshots, no legal disclaimers on first touch.
6. **Word count:** 80–130 words maximum for the body. Under 100 is ideal.

---

## PERSONALIZATION REQUIREMENTS

Every email must reference at least ONE of the following:
- Their company's specific number of employees
- Their specific trade/industry
- Their specific city or WNY location
- A specific detail from their website, LinkedIn, or job postings
- Years they've been in business (if notable)
- A recent hire, award, or news mention

**Generic emails that could be sent to anyone get zero replies. Specific emails that prove you did 60 seconds of research get conversations.**

## REPLY HANDLING

**If they reply "not interested":**
"Completely understood — appreciate you getting back to me. If anything comes up on the HR or hiring side down the road, I'm local and easy to reach. Take care."

**If they reply "how does this work?":**
Send the link: protect.waymarkhrgroup.com + one sentence: "Takes 90 seconds, shows you exactly where [Company] stands on the 10 areas NY State looks at first."

**If they reply "what do you charge?":**
"Depends on what you need. The free check at protect.waymarkhrgroup.com is a good starting point — shows what's actually missing. From there we'd have a 30-minute call and I'll tell you exactly what it would take and what it costs. No pitch, just facts."

**If they reply "send me more info":**
"Happy to — want me to send the link to the free check first? It'll show you exactly where your gaps are and we can talk through what makes sense from there."

## WHAT THIS SKILL IS NOT FOR

- Writing emails that go to general lists without personalization
- Writing emails that promise specific results ("you'll save $50K")
- Writing emails that ask for meetings on the first touch
- Writing emails that include the landing page link in the first message
- Writing emails longer than 130 words in the body
- Writing emails that use HTML templates, logos, or tracked links
